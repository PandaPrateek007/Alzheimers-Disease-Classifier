What is Alzheimer's Disease?

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects the brain and leads to a gradual decline in cognitive and memory functions. It is the most common cause of dementia among older adults.

Causes of Alzheimer's Disease

The exact cause of Alzheimer's disease is not fully understood, but it is believed to be a result of a combination of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors. The buildup of abnormal protein deposits in the brain, including beta-amyloid plaques and tau tangles, is associated with the disease's progression.

Treatment of Alzheimer's Disease

As of now, there is no cure for Alzheimer's disease, and treatment focuses on managing symptoms, slowing down the progression, and improving the quality of life for affected individuals. Medications, cognitive stimulation, and support from caregivers are often utilized in managing the disease.

Categories of Alzheimer's Disease

"Demented" is a term used to describe individuals experiencing a state of cognitive decline and memory impairment, commonly associated with conditions like Alzheimer's disease. Based on the severity of symptoms and the decline in cognitive abilities Alzheimer's disease is often classified into following four stages: Non-Demented, Very Mild Demented , Mild Demented , Moderate Demented.
